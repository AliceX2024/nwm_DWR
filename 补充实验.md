In our original implementation, no explicit warm-up phase was used (start_steps=0). The memory bank was initialized with random noise, but it was rapidly overwritten by real features during training. Specifically, with a bank size of 2048 and 128 newly inserted features per training step, the bank became fully populated after 16 steps only. Therefore, the influence of random initialization was restricted to a very short transient stage. To further address the reviewer’s concern, we additionally analyzed the convergence behavior during a warm-up setting and observed that the loss remained stable while the bank was being progressively populated before reweighting was activated.
